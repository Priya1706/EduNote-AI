**Title**
The East India Company: From Trading Rights to Territorial Power (1600-1858)

**Definitions**

* Diwani rights: rights granted by Shah Alam, the Mughal Emperor, to the East India Company in 1765, allowing them to collect taxes and exercise civil functions in Bengal, Pihar, and Odisha.
* Exclusive trading rights: granted by Queen Elizabeth I to the East India Company in 1600, giving them the sole right to trade in the eastern region.

**Key Points**

• 1600: The East India Company gets exclusive trading rights from Queen Elizabeth I.
• 1608: The company arrives in India due to its rich resources and high demand for Indian products in Europe, Middle East, and other regions.
• 1764-1765: The battle of Bucks leads to the East India Company gaining Diwani rights over Bengal, Pihar, and Odisha.
• 1857: The revolt against the East India Company's rule marks the beginning of the end of its territorial power.

**Important Years/Facts**

* 1600: Queen Elizabeth I grants exclusive trading rights to the East India Company.
* 1608: The company arrives in India.
* 1765: The East India Company gains Diwani rights over Bengal, Pihar, and Odisha.
* 1857: The revolt against the East India Company's rule.

**Conclusion**
The East India Company's transformation from a trading company to a territorial power was marked by significant milestones, including the granting of exclusive trading rights in 1600 and Diwani rights in 1765. However, its rule came under scrutiny following the 1857 revolt, ultimately leading to the transfer of power to the British crown in 1858.
