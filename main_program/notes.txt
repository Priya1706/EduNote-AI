Black Holes:

**Definitions:**

* A black hole is a region in space where gravity is so strong that not even light can escape from it, making it invisible to the naked eye.
* A black hole is formed when very massive stars run out of fuel and collapse under their own gravitational pull.

**Key Points:**

• Black holes are characterized by an extremely strong gravitational pull, known as a singularity, where the mass is concentrated and density increases.
• Nothing, including light, can escape the event horizon, which is the boundary beyond which nothing, not even light, can escape the black hole's gravitational force.
• Black holes form when massive stars exhaust their fuel and collapse under their own gravity, causing them to become incredibly dense and compact.

* The existence of black holes was first proposed by Albert Einstein in his theory of general relativity in 1915.
