**Title:** The Emergence of East India Company as a Territorial Power

**Definitions:**

* Diwani rights: Exclusive rights to collect revenue and exercise civil functions in a region, granted by the Mughal Emperor Shah Alam.
* Trading rights: Exclusive permission to trade with certain regions or countries.

**Key Points:**

• 1600: East India Company (EIC) received exclusive trading rights from Queen Elizabeth I.
• 1608: EIC arrived in India due to its rich resources and high demand for Indian products in Europe, the Middle East, and elsewhere.
• 1764-1765: EIC fought the Battle of Plassey, gaining Diwani rights over Bengal, Bihar, and Odisha (the most fertile region at that time).
• 1857: The Sepoy Mutiny led to the British Crown taking control from the East India Company.

**Important Years/Facts:**

* 1600: EIC received exclusive trading rights
* 1765: Battle of Plassey and Diwani rights granted to EIC
* 1858: British Crown took control from the East India Company

**Conclusion:**

The East India Company's emergence as a territorial power was marked by significant events, including gaining exclusive trading rights, fighting battles to expand its influence, and eventually losing control to the British Crown.
