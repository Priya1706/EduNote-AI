**The Emergence of East India Company**

**Definitions:**

* **Diwani rights:** The right to collect taxes and perform civil functions, granted by Shah Alam the Mughal Emperor in 1765.
* **Exclusive trading rights:** The right given to the East India Company by Queen Elizabeth I in 1600 to trade only with the eastern side of the world.

**Key Points:**

• The East India Company was granted exclusive trading rights in 1600, allowing them to trade only with the eastern side of the world.
• In 1608, the company arrived in India due to its rich resources and high demand for Indian products in Europe, Middle East, and other parts of the world.
• The company won a major battle (Battle of Plassey) in 1764 and was granted Diwani rights over Bengal, Pihar, and Odisha in 1765 by Shah Alam the Mughal Emperor.
• The EIC became a territorial power from this point onwards, collecting revenue and performing civil functions until 1858.

**Important Years/Facts:**

* 1600: East India Company granted exclusive trading rights
* 1608: EIC arrives in India
* 1764: Battle of Plassey (major victory for the company)
* 1765: Diwani rights granted to EIC over Bengal, Pihar, and Odisha
* 1857: Revolt against British rule leads to the takeover of the company's rule by the British crown in 1858

**Conclusion:**

The East India Company's emergence as a territorial power was marked by its exclusive trading rights, major battles, and grant of Diwani rights. This ultimately led to the takeover of the company's rule by the British crown in 1858.
